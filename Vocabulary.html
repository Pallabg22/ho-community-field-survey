Name Some Musical Instruments of the Ho People

Music plays an important role in the social, cultural, and ritual life of the Ho community. The following are some traditional musical instruments commonly used by the Ho people:

  • Nagara / Dhamsha – A large kettle drum used during festivals, rituals, and community gatherings.
  • Madal – A double-headed drum made of clay or wood, widely used in folk music and dance.
  • Ghungur – Small bells often worn around the ankles, especially during traditional dances.
  • Rut-tu – A traditional bamboo flute used for melodic music and cultural performances.

These are some of the traditional musical instruments of the Ho people that are still used in cultural events and social ceremonies.
