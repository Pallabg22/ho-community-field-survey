Birth Ceremony

In the Ho community, childbirth is followed by a series of traditional rituals. After the birth of a child, the midwife is called, and a period of ritual impurity known as “Ashouch” is observed. This period lasts for four days.

On the seventh day, the newborn’s nails and hair are cut, and the clothes are cleaned as part of the purification process. On the twenty-first day, the baby is formally introduced to the community. On this occasion, a chicken is sacrificed in the name of Dharam Thakur and Singbonga Thakur, the Sun God, seeking blessings for the child’s health and future.

Marriage Rules

Child marriage is not practiced in Ho society. When boys and girls reach marriageable age, marriages are generally arranged by parents according to community norms.

Marriage within the same clan is strictly prohibited. If such a marriage takes place, the couple is expelled from the society. After marriage, the newly married couple usually does not continue living in the same village.

Like other Austric groups, the Ho community follows the custom of levirate marriage, where if an elder brother dies, his wife may be married by the younger brother.
